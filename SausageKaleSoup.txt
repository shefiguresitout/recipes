Sausage-Kale Soup

3 cloves garlic
1 large onion
2 carrots
1 pound sweet or hot Italian chicken, turkey, or pork sausage
2 tablespoons olive oil
Salt and black pepper, to taste
1 teaspoon crushed red pepper, or to taste
1 cup white wine
6 cups chicken stock
1 can (15 ounces) chopped tomatoes
¼ pound wedge Parmesan cheese, rind removed, cheese grated
? cup barley
1 large bunch of kale (about 3 cups)
1 can (15 ounces) red kidney beans

1.  Crush garlic

2.  Cut carrots into chunks 

3.  Chop onions
4.  Remove sausage from casing
5.  In a soup pot over medium heat, heat the olive oil. Add the sausage and cook, breaking up the meat 
with a spoon, until it is lightly browned.

6.  Stir in the onion, carrots, salt, black pepper, and red pepper. Cook, stirring often, for 5 minutes, or 
until the vegetables begin to soften. 

7.  Add the garlic. Cook for 1 minute more.

8.  Turn up the heat to medium­high. Add the wine. Cook for 3 minutes, or until the wine reduces by 
half.

9.   Add the stock, tomatoes, barley, and cheese rind. Bring to a boil, lower the heat, and set the cover 
askew. Simmer for 30 minutes.

10.  Remove leaves from kale and chop coarsely

11.  Add the kale and simmer for 10 minutes more, or until the kale is tender.

12.  Add the beans and simmer for 2 minutes, or until the beans are hot. 

Total simmering time is 52 minutes. Remove and discard the cheese rind from the soup. Ladle into bowls 
and sprinkle with grated Parmesan.
